## 1. Bridge Crossing Puzzle  
**Minimum time: 17 minutes**

**Explanation:**  
Optimal steps:  
1 & 2 cross (2), 1 returns (1), 5 & 10 cross (10), 2 returns (2), 1 & 2 cross (2).  
Total = **17 minutes**.

## 2. Lightbulb Switch Puzzle  
**Solution:**  
Turn switch 1 on for 5 minutes → turn it off → turn switch 2 on → enter room.  
- ON bulb = switch 2  
- OFF but warm = switch 1  
- OFF & cold = switch 3

## 3. Poisoned Wine Puzzle  
**Answer:** Use binary testing with 10 people.  
Each person drinks bottles whose number has a 1-bit in their binary label.  
Dead/alive pattern identifies the exact bottle.  
10 people give 2¹⁰ = 1024 combinations → enough for 1000 bottles.

## 4. Wolf, Goat & Cabbage Puzzle  
**Solution:**  
Goat over → return → wolf over → goat back → cabbage over → return → goat over.

## 5. Counterfeit Coin Puzzle  
**Answer:** 2 weighings.  
1st: 3 vs 3.  
If equal → fake is in remaining 3.  
2nd: weigh 1 vs 1 → lighter is fake, or remaining one is fake.

## 6. Two Eggs & 100 Floors Puzzle  
**Answer:** 14 drops worst case.  
Explanation: Drop from floors 14, 27, 39, 50, 60, 69, 77, 84, 90, 95, 99, 100…  
Using decreasing intervals gives worst case = 14.  
(14×15)/2 = 105 ≥ 100.

---

## 7. Blue Eyes Island Puzzle  
**Answer:** All 100 leave on night 100.  
Explanation:  
If 1 person → leaves night 1.  
If 2 → leave night 2.  
If N → all leave night N.  
By induction, 100 leave on night 100.

---

## 8. Rope Burning Puzzle  
**Answer:** 45 minutes.  
Light rope A at both ends and rope B at one end.  
A burns in 30 minutes.  
Then light other end of B → remaining 30 mins burn in 15 mins.  
Total = **45 mins**.

## 9. Missing £1 Puzzle  
**Answer:** There is no missing £1.  
Guests paid £27 total:  
£25 to hotel + £2 to bellboy.  
Puzzle incorrectly adds the £2 on top of £27, causing the illusion.

## 10. Chessboard Grain Puzzle

≈ **18 quintillion**.  
This demonstrates exponential growth.
